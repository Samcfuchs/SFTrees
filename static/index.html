San Francisco Trees

Many of the trees in the San Francisco Trees dataset have the date of their planting recorded. For these trees, we can calculate almost exactly their age. This allows us to examine the relationship between tree age and size--essentially, the growth of trees over time. While the data is of course noisy (the size of a tree at its planting can vary wildly), we can clearly see a pattern when we examine the mean diameter for trees planted in each year. This visualization allows us to watch trees grow over time.

The two diagrams in the right-hand column depict two subsets of the data. In the map on the top right, a map is drawn showing the locations of the largest trees around San Francisco. Many of these are recognizable as the outliers in the scatterplot on the left. By showing this plot, we gain a better understanding of those outliers and how they are distributed throughout the city. We can imagine a San Francisco resident who looks at the plot on the left and immediately wonders at the fact that there are trees in the city that are nearly a meter in diameter. This plot would show them where those trees are, perhaps so they see them when they are nearby.

Finally, we show a detail view of the densest area of the scatterplot, below 25-30 cm. This is important because in the first visualization, the outliers make the pattern of trees growing with age difficult to parse immediately. In this innset pattern, we remove the subset of the data greater than some size_cutoff so that the plot lies entirely within the range of (0,25]. Additionally, I adjust the smoothing function applied to the average line so that the random variation from year to year is less pronounced.

I chose to arrange these two detail plots on the right vertically so that they correspond visually with the data subsets they represent. I would like them to match the subset division line on the left exactly, but I find that such a division is too imbalanced, and thisi visual inbalance outweighs the slight visual load incurred by the audience in making sense of this separation.
